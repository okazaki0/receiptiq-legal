1. Information we collect

1.1 Information you provide

  • Account information. When you create an account, we collect your email address and a password (the password is hashed and stored by our authentication provider; we never see it in plain text).
  • Profile information. Your display name, if you choose to set one in Settings.
  • Receipt data. Vendor name, amount, currency, date, expense category, optional notes, and the receipt photo. You enter or capture this data; we do not generate it from external sources.
  • Mileage data. Trip start/end timestamps, GPS coordinates of the route, total miles, and an optional purpose label (e.g. "Client Meeting"). Recorded only while you actively use the in-app drive tracker.
  • Support communications. If you email us, we receive your email address and the contents of your message.

1.2 Information collected automatically

  • Usage limits and counters. A daily count of receipt scans and AI-deduction calls per user, used to enforce per-day caps. Stored locally on your device.
  • Device identifier (fallback only). When you are temporarily signed out (for example, during the brief window between sign-out and sign-in), we use your device's vendor identifier to keep the daily-cap counter consistent. We do not transmit this identifier to our servers.
  • Advertising identifiers. If you have not purchased the "Remove Ads" upgrade, the AdMob SDK may collect your device's advertising identifier and limited ad-interaction data, subject to your consent (see Advertising).
  • Crash and diagnostic information. If we add crash reporting in a future update, this section will describe what is collected.

1.3 Information we do not collect

  • We do not access your contacts, calendar, microphone, or SMS.
  • We do not access your bank account, credit card, or other financial accounts. ReceiptIQ does not connect to any financial institution.
  • We do not collect biometric data.
  • We do not track your location in the background. The GPS feature only runs while you are actively recording a drive in the foreground.

2. How we use information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Provide the core features of the app: storing receipts and mileage entries, generating reports, syncing your data across your devices.
  • Authenticate you when you sign in.
  • Provide AI-assisted features (receipt parsing and deduction analysis) — see AI processing.
  • Enforce daily usage limits on AI features so the app stays affordable for everyone.
  • Process your one-time "Remove Ads" purchase via Apple App Store or Google Play.
  • Show advertisements (if you have not purchased Remove Ads) — see Advertising.
  • Communicate with you about account issues, support requests, and material changes to the app.
  • Comply with legal obligations and prevent abuse, fraud, or security incidents.

3. AI processing

ReceiptIQ uses two AI components. They have very different privacy properties; this section explains both.

3.1 On-device OCR (no transmission)

When you scan a receipt, the app first uses Google ML Kit Text Recognition to convert the receipt photo into raw text. This step runs entirely on your device. The image and the recognized text are not transmitted to Google or to us during this step.

3.2 Cloud AI (Google Gemini)

The recognized text is then sent to Google Gemini Flash Lite, an AI service operated by Google, to extract structured fields (vendor, amount, date, category) from it. The same Gemini service is used by the optional AI Deduction Coach to analyze whether a receipt qualifies as a tax deduction.

What we send to Gemini:

  • The OCR text from your receipt photo (no image is uploaded).
  • For deduction analysis: the receipt's vendor, amount, currency, category, date, and any notes you entered.

What we do not send to Gemini:

  • Your name, email, account ID, or any other identifier that would link the request back to you.
  • Your photo of the receipt.
  • Your location, device ID, or any other device-level information.

Google's processing of data sent to the Gemini API is governed by the Gemini API Additional Terms. Google states that data sent to the paid Gemini API is not used to train Google's foundation models.

To minimize repeat calls, the AI's answer for each receipt is cached locally on your device (and synced to your account in our cloud database) keyed by a hash of the receipt's content. Editing the receipt invalidates the cache and triggers a fresh call.

5. Data sharing & sale

We do not sell your personal information. We do not "share" your personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising as that term is defined under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), except as disclosed under Advertising.

We share information with third parties only:

  • To provide the core services described above (e.g. Supabase to store your receipts, Gemini to analyze them).
  • When you explicitly direct us to (e.g. you tap "Share" on a receipt to send it via your phone's share sheet).
  • To comply with legal process, a lawful subpoena, or a government request.
  • To protect our rights, prevent fraud or abuse, or investigate violations of our terms.
  • In connection with a merger, acquisition, or asset sale, in which case the acquiring entity will be bound by this policy or will provide notice of any changes.

6. Advertising

If you have not purchased the "Remove Ads" upgrade, the app displays banner and interstitial advertisements via Google AdMob.

Consent. On first launch, we present the Google User Messaging Platform (UMP) consent flow. In regions where consent is required (the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, Switzerland, and CCPA-eligible US states), you can accept, reject, or customize the data uses Google AdMob applies. Your choice persists across launches; you can change it later in Settings.

What AdMob may collect. Subject to your consent: an advertising identifier, ad interaction data (which ad you saw, whether you tapped it), IP address, coarse location, and device characteristics. AdMob's collection is governed by Google's privacy policy and is independent of your ReceiptIQ account.

How to remove ads completely. Settings → Remove Ads → one-time purchase. Once purchased, no ad requests are made and no ad-related data is collected by ReceiptIQ on your device.

Tracking transparency on iOS. On iOS 14.5+, you may also be presented with the App Tracking Transparency prompt. Choosing "Ask App Not to Track" limits AdMob to non-personalized ads only.

7. Location data

ReceiptIQ accesses your location only while you are actively recording a drive using the in-app mileage tracker. The app does not access your location in the background, before you tap Start Drive, or after you tap End Drive.

Location data we record:

  • Start and end coordinates of the drive.
  • The polyline of GPS points captured during the drive (for the live route map and total-miles calculation).

This data is stored on your device and, if you are signed in, synced to your Supabase-hosted account. It is never transmitted to AdMob, Gemini, or any other third party. You can delete a drive at any time from the Mileage tab.

9. Data retention

  • Local data. Your receipts and mileage entries are stored on your device until you delete them, uninstall the app, or wipe the local database via Settings.
  • Cloud data. If you are signed in, the same data is synced to our Supabase-hosted database under your account. It is retained until you delete the data or your account.
  • Backups. Supabase performs operational backups of the database. Deleted records may persist in encrypted backups for up to 30 days before being permanently expunged.
  • AI request logs. Google retains Gemini API request logs per its own policies. We do not retain a separate log of AI requests on our side.
  • Account deletion. See Account & data deletion.

10. Security

We take reasonable measures to protect your information:

  • All network traffic between the app and our servers is encrypted in transit using HTTPS / TLS.
  • Receipt photos uploaded to cloud storage are accessible only via short-lived signed URLs scoped to your account.
  • Passwords are hashed by our authentication provider; we do not have access to your plain-text password.
  • Cloud storage and database access is restricted to authenticated requests, scoped per user via row-level security policies.
  • API keys for third-party services are never embedded in the app's source code in a recoverable form.

No method of electronic transmission or storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security, and you use the app at your own risk.

12. Account & data deletion

You can delete your account and all associated data at any time:

  1. In-app: Settings → Account → Delete Account. (If self-service deletion has not yet been enabled in your version of the app, follow option 2.)
  2. Email request: Email receiptiq.support@gmail.com with the subject "Account deletion request" from the email address associated with your account. We will permanently delete your account and all cloud data within seven (7) days. Local data on your device will be wiped on next launch (or when you uninstall the app).

Once deleted, your data cannot be recovered.
